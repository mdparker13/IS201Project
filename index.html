Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein

Fun Facts

  1. Germany
    • There are more than 20,000 castles in Germany. (Here is a video of some of the most popular castles.)
    • Beer is considered food in the Bavaria region.
    • There are more than 1,000 kinds of sausages in Germany.
    • 65% of the highways in Germany have no speed limit.

  2. Switzerland
    • Switzerland has four official languages.
    • The Alps make up almost 60% of the country.
    • It has 7,000 lakes.
    • 25% of the population is foreign.

  3. Austria
    • It contains the worlds largest ice cave, which extends for almost 42 kilometers.
    • Even though it is small, it shares borders with eight other countries.
    • The austrian flag is one of the oldest national flags in the world.
    • Vienna has the oldest zoo in the world which was founded in 1752.

  4. Liechtenstein
    • It is the 4th smallest and 2nd least visted country in Europe.
    • It has an area of only 62 square miles, and is nearly 8 times smaller than the city of Los Angeles.
    • Dentures are one of its primary exports.
    • It was the last European country to allow women to vote.



The german speaking countries are central in Europe, and are also fairly average in their pollotion contributions.
